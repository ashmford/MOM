04  Our mission

We exist to help noncustodial parents create stable, accountable, and productive lives.

Mending Our Mistakes is an Arkansas-based nonprofit providing case navigation, employment support, legal guidance, and peer support for parents working to reconnect with their children.

The population
Parents facing barriers tied to employment, housing, transportation, legal navigation, and child support compliance.
Two pathways
Both DHS-adjacent parents and private family court parents hit the same wall. MOM serves both.
The gap
Existing enforcement structures focus on collection — not the conditions that make compliance possible.

05  What we do

A coordinated continuum of support.

Eight services, delivered as a coordinated model — not a menu. Parents receive what they need, when they need it, with a navigator guiding every step.

01

Parenting Education

Evidence-based curricula tied to court and reunification goals.

02

Peer Support

Group and one-on-one support from people with lived experience.

03

Legal Navigation

Pro se support, license restoration, order modification.

04

Workforce Development

Job readiness, placement, and retention coaching.

05

Life Skills Training

Housing readiness, healthcare access, daily stabilization.

06

Supervised Visitation

Safe, structured time with children during reconnection.

07

Financial Education

Budgeting, arrears management, banking access.

08

Transportation Assistance

Getting to work, court, treatment, and visits.
