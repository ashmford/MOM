About Mending Our Mistakes

Our mission

We exist to help noncustodial parents create stable, accountable, and productive lives.

We exist to help noncustodial parents create stable, accountable, and productive lives so they can remain meaningfully connected to their children.

Our vision

We envision communities where noncustodial parents have a fair chance to rebuild stability, stay connected to their children, and create healthier futures for their families — where support and structure replace isolation, setbacks, and preventable family separation.

The gap in the system

The system enforces. We stabilize.

Arkansas's existing structures — child support enforcement, DHS case management, family court — are built around compliance and consequences, not around the conditions that make compliance possible.

Parents navigating these systems face suspended licenses, unaffordable orders, housing instability, and no legal guidance. When formal involvement ends, there is no coordinated support waiting on the other side. That moment — when the system steps back but stability hasn't arrived — is where MOM works.

Both DHS-adjacent parents and private family court parents encounter the same gap. MOM serves both.

How we think

Support and structure, not isolation and consequences.

Mending Our Mistakes is a navigation system — one that walks alongside parents who are working toward stability and helps them get there. That means case-by-case coordination, showing up at court dates and job interviews and supervised visits, financial education that accounts for arrears most parents never had anyone help them understand, and peer support from people who have sat in the same courtroom.

Stability is something you build. The model works because it's designed around that reality.

Every dollar goes directly to a parent working to get back to their kids.

Mending Our Mistakes operates on a lean model. Your gift funds case navigation, emergency transportation, supervised visitation, and the wraparound support that makes reunification possible.
