    The kind of partner we're looking for.

    MOM works because no single organization can do this alone. We're building a coordinated network across legal advocacy, supervised visitation, and health and stabilization services.

    Partnership

    Building the network that makes this work.

    Noncustodial parents navigating family court face barriers that no single service can address alone. MOM coordinates across legal, housing, health, and community systems to give parents a real pathway to stability. Our partners make that coordination possible.

    If your organization serves families in Garland, Hot Spring, Saline, or Grant County and wants to work alongside us, reach out to admin@mendingourmistakes.org.

    01 — Legal & Advocacy

    Supporting parents through the system.

    Family law firms willing to provide limited-scope representation or "Know Your Rights" workshops. Mediation services that specialize in high-conflict custody cases and can help parents reach agreements outside of a judge's ruling. Paternity and child support specialists who can help noncustodial parents understand and navigate the financial logistics of the system.

    02 — Supervised Visitation

    Creating safe spaces for families.

    Visitation centers that provide safe, neutral, child-friendly environments for supervised visits. Public libraries and community centers interested in establishing MOM Corners — safe, public meeting spots for families. Therapeutic supervised visitation providers whose mental health professionals can oversee visits and provide real-time support.

    03 — Health & Stabilization

    Building the foundation for everything else.

    Trauma-informed counseling centers that can provide priority referrals for parents dealing with CPTSD or postpartum trauma. Transitional housing programs — especially those with reunification stage options where children can stay overnight as the parent progresses. Mobile health clinics that can provide low-cost physicals or dental care, which are often overlooked barriers to employment and court compliance.
